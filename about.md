# About the tracker

The Tennessee Digital Rights Tracker exists to help ordinary people understand what a bill, policy, court ruling, contract, or government technology system actually does.

It is not a campaign database, an intelligence-gathering operation, or a place to store stories about identifiable people.

## Editorial approach

Each entry distinguishes among:

- **Primary-source fact**
- **Official claim**
- **Advocacy interpretation**
- **Project analysis**
- **Uncertainty**
- **Prediction**

The project should be willing to say, “We do not know yet.”

## Research and legal limitations

This tracker is based on publicly available records and does not provide legal advice or a binding legal opinion.

The project cannot guarantee that an agency has disclosed every internal implementation detail, and it cannot independently inspect or verify nonpublic systems, security controls, access logs, retention practices, or internal data-sharing arrangements. The absence of a public document does not prove that no internal policy or safeguard exists.

Entries identify these evidence gaps rather than guessing. Strong legal, privacy, medical, or technical conclusions should be reviewed by an appropriately qualified attorney or subject-matter expert.

## Corrections

Corrections should be documented in the entry’s revision history and linked to the issue or pull request that produced the change.
